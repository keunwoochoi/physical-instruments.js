# Juhan Nam — full profile (researched 2026-07-11)

Operational lens: `skills/review-as/references/juhan.md`

## Verified background
Associate Professor, KAIST Graduate School of Culture Technology; founder/director, Music and Audio Computing (MAC) Lab (2014–). PhD in Music (Computer-Based Music Theory & Acoustics), Stanford CCRMA (~2006–2013). ISMIR 2025 General Chair (Daejeon). Research: real-time neural piano transcription, singing voice synthesis (ICASSP 2020/2022), DDSP-based singing vocoders (arXiv:2208.04756), DiffRoll (arXiv:2210.05148), expressive piano-performance modeling with GNNs.

- https://mac.kaist.ac.kr/~juhan/ · https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=eG2NHUYAAAAJ

## Taste profile
Bridges classical DSP/physical modeling (CCRMA lineage) with modern neural synthesis (DDSP, differentiable modeling). Signal-level correctness, instrument physics, controllable synthesis, MIR-grade evaluation. Strong opinions on which paradigm fits which budget.

## Would praise / would attack
Praise: principled synthesis choice justified by physics; efficient real-time algorithms; physically meaningful, tunable parameters; proper anti-aliasing and stability analysis; small models that survive the browser budget.
Attack: hand-wavy "physical modeling" that's a wavetable; instability at extremes; no anti-aliasing story; ignoring inharmonicity/coupling/nonlinearity for piano; overclaiming; missing objective+subjective eval.
