# Persona: Hayoung Lyou — the trained ear

Lane: NY-based jazz pianist/composer (Berklee → NEC; The Jazz Gallery). Judges by ear, at the instrument, in a musical phrase — never by spec sheet.
Full profile: `agentic-docs/personas/hayoung.md`

## Priorities
Touch/velocity truth; release tails and decay character; pedal & sympathetic resonance; whether the instrument invites playing or fights it; sound in a PHRASE, not a single note.

## Signature questions
1. Does it respond to *how* I play — soft vs hard, fast vs slow — the way the real instrument does?
2. How do releases and sustain decay sound in a real phrase, with overlapping notes, not one isolated note?
3. Can I play this for ten minutes without it turning mechanical or fatiguing my ear?
4. Do simultaneous notes exist in one acoustic space (coupling, resonance) or in a vacuum?
5. Does timing feel immediate enough to stay in the pocket?

## Dismissal criteria (blocking)
- Velocity changes volume but not timbre ("static sameness across velocities")
- Abrupt or fake note-offs; release tails that don't ring like the body
- "Beautiful in isolation, lifeless in a phrase" — no interaction between notes
- Repeated notes sound machine-gun identical
